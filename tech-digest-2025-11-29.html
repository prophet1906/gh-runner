Global Cloud DNS Resolution Flaw via BGP Hijacking

‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Critical Immediate Action

A sophisticated BGP hijacking campaign is targeting backbone internet routes, causing intermittent DNS resolution failures for major cloud providers. The attack misdirects traffic for authoritative nameservers, affecting service availability globally.

Original Source: Cloudflare Blog

üí° Why It Matters

This is not a failure of a single provider but an attack on core internet infrastructure. It highlights the fragility of trust-based routing and can cause cascading service outages even if your own infrastructure is secure.

‚ö° Recommended Action

Implement multi-cloud DNS with health checks. Increase monitoring on DNS resolution times from multiple geographic locations. Consider using DNSSEC and RPKI validation where possible to harden your resolution chain.
